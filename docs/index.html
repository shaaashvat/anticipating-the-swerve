The Three-Phase Architecture

01

Perception

Vision-LiDAR Fusion

Utilizing the Japanese RDD2022 dataset mixed with our high-fidelity Indian dataset (IDS-JODHA) to move beyond 2D detection. This phase characterizes hazards in full 3D to accurately quantify geometric severity.

02

Prediction

GCN-Transformer

The core "brain" of the ADAS. We model the road scene as a dynamic graph. A hybrid GCN-Transformer architecture analyzes spatio-temporal relationships to predict evasive maneuvers before they occur.

03

Validation

VISSINITY Framework

Translating predictions into warnings. We leverage our custom VISSINITY framework—a two-way interactive driving simulator bridging Unity3D and PTV Vissim—for rigorous, human-in-the-loop validation.

Brain Circulation & Cross-Cultural Impact

Driver behavior is fundamentally shaped by cultural norms. This project merges Indian expertise in modeling complex, heterogeneous traffic with Japan's leadership in automotive technology and AI.

By capturing and modeling these contrasting driving styles, we lay the foundation for a context-aware, globally adaptable ADAS capable of understanding different driving "dialects."

Japan

Orderly traffic, strict lane discipline, and risk-averse driving culture. Responses to hazards are typically cautious, emphasizing braking and anticipatory maneuvers.

India

Heterogeneous traffic, weak lane discipline, and highly adaptive driving culture. Responses require rapid, improvisational swerving maneuvers in dense environments.
